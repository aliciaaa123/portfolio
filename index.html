About Me

I'm a multilingual public health researcher trained both as a dentist and a public health scientist. I hold a Bachelor of Dental Surgery (BDS) and an MS in Public Health Research focused on Translational and Implementation Science from Boston University.

What drives me is the gap between what research finds and what actually reaches patients. I've worked across NIH-funded clinical studies, hospital coordination, surgical care, and healthcare data analytics — always asking how evidence can be more useful in the real world.

I care deeply about health equity and have spent much of my career working with underserved communities, from rural screening campaigns in India to diverse patient populations right here in Boston.
